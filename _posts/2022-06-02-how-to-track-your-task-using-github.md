1. Create a repository for <b>gsoc2022</b> in your GitHub repository. 
2. Go to the <i>Projects</i> tab and press <i>Add project</i>.
<br>
<img src="/img/gsoc/01.png" width="600" height="250">
<br>
3. Go to your profile to create a new project and choose <i>Create project</i>. It will lead you to the corresponding page.
<br>
<img src="/img/gsoc/02.png" width="600" height="100">
<br>
4. Rename your project and add your mentors and team members in <i>Settings</i> -> <i>Manage access</i>.
5. You should go back to the <i>Projects</i> tab and once you press <i>Add project</i> your Project should appear.
It takes a bit longer for the Project to be generated and to appear in the dropdown list.
6. Now, go to the <i>Issues</i> tab and add a new issue that will be linked to the Project.
<br>
<img src="/img/gsoc/03.png" width="600" height="100">
<br>
7. As you can see below, you can name the issue, add more details, assign it to yourself or your mentors, label it 
(there are several predefined categories), and add it to your newly created Project!
<br>
<img src="/img/gsoc/04.png" width="600" height="250">
<br>
8. If you check your Project's page, you will see your added task. Think of this as the main dashboard that shows the progress
of your to-do lists.
<br>
<img src="/img/gsoc/05.png" width="600" height="100">
<br>

Good luck in organising your tasks 😊

To read more about the beta version, follow this [link](https://docs.github.com/en/issues/trying-out-the-new-projects-experience/about-projects).
