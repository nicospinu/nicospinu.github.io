The PhD project focused on advancing the quantitative Adverse Outcome Pathway (qAOP) type of computational model. 
This was achieved by assessing the state-of-the-art and providing improved methodologies to integrate the variety of mechanistic data. The ultimate goal was to support chemical risk assessment with better ways of evaluating the toxicity in humans as a result of chemical exposure, e.g., use of cosmetics, chemical industry, and medicines.
<br>
#### AOPs, AOP networks and qAOP
Proposed a decade ago, the <b>adverse outcome pathway (AOP)</b> framework serves as a knowledge assembly, evaluation, interpretation, and communication tool, designed to support pathway-oriented chemical risk assessment. It relies on systematic literature review, data mining etc to map the potential mechanism of toxicity of a chemical or other stressors such as a virus.
<br>
AOPs are linear at their origins while biology is complex and nonlinear. Thus, <b>networks of AOPs</b> are constructed to represent the biological processes to identify interactions and common events to be further studied and analysed.
<br>
<b>qAOP</b> is a mathematical model that describes the causal relationships summarised in an AOP with the aim to quantify the tipping points, i.e., when a chemical (or another stressor) exceeds the threshold of inducing the toxicity at cellular, tissue, organ and population levels.
<br>
#### Relevance
* We are exposed daily to chemical substances for most of which the underlying mechanisms of toxicity remain unknown and/or not sufficiently studied.
* The available evidence is heterogeneous and there is a lack of integrative approaches to exploit existing historical information and <i>in silico</i> and <i>in vitro</i> data.
* The EU legislation on cosmetics from 2008 does not allow for the new substances to be tested on animals but still, the safety has to be proven. Thus, novel alternative methods such as computational models and stem cells models are proposed but their applicability and validation remain to be demonstrated.
* Drug discovery is fragmented while safety is the second reason for the failure of a molecule candidate to become a medicinal product.
* The European Green Deal approved 2020 along with the Sustainable Development Goals, are stimulating and encouraging for efforts directed toward the development of novel integrative approaches to decipher, understand the toxicity potency and prevent any harmful chemicals to be put on the market.
* Neurotoxicity is one of the most controversial and complicated endpoints to be studied, e.g., due to ethical reasons compared to other organs such as skin or liver. Our brain is continuously developing until we are 30 years old. Exposing our brain to harmful chemicals, especially during this perioud of life, increases the risk to develop cognitive dysfunctions including Alzheimer's disease and Parkinson's disease or ADHD in children.

#### Main outcomes
* The Bayesian model is ready to be taken further, but also can be improved and adapted to evaluate chemical substances for their potency of inducing neurotoxicity.
* The PhD thesis served as the main source for the preparation of the Framework document on the topic of qAOP as part of the newly EU-funded project RISK-HUNT3R.
