<h4><b>Some other thoughts</b></h4>
I was told that it is about being the right person at the right time. Who knows, it might have been so. But I can argue that no matter what, it is also about the <b>person</b>, the spirit, motivation and willingness to spend 100% of the time, and sometimes even more, on the research topic; the <b>resources</b>, e.g., the scholarship itself offers a decent life with the note that it depends on the country where you are mainly located as the prices have escalated everywhere these days; and the <b>teams</b>, exactly multiple teams each with its role. Think of it as a puzzle: science is multidisciplinary.
<br>
A couple of months ago, I was asked if everything that my PhD has was developed by me. I initially thought it to be a joke. Then, I took it as a tricky question asked to test me but surprisingly, someone can indeed, find it to have been a lot for someone to carry so much in less than three and a half years (four to six years is the average). Still, I am not the one to judge and will leave it to the others. This reminded me that it is difficult to appreciate your work, and each has a personal perspective on things. 
<br>
<br>
<b>Whatever you choose, follow it wholeheartedly and appreciate the journey itself 🦋 </b>
